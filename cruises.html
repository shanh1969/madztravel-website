Cruise Vacations — From Caribbean Escapes to Alaskan Adventures

Unpack once and wake up somewhere new. Madz Travel matches you to the right ship, cabin and itinerary — and secures perks and pricing you won't find booking direct.

Cruising is one of the best-value ways to see the world: your hotel, meals, entertainment and transportation travel with you. But cruise lines, ships and cabin categories vary enormously — the right choice for a first-timer is very different from a seasoned cruiser's, and an advisor makes sure you get it right the first time.

Types of Cruises We Plan

Caribbean

Warm-weather sailing year-round from Florida and beyond — perfect for families and first-time cruisers.

Alaska

Glaciers, whales and wilderness. A bucket-list summer voyage best booked early for the best cabins.

Europe & Mediterranean

Multiple iconic cities in one trip — Italy, Greece, Spain and more, without repacking.

River Cruises

The Rhine, Danube and Seine up close — intimate ships, all-inclusive fares and scenic sailing.

  • The right cabin. Location on the ship affects noise, motion and price — we know which decks and categories are worth it.

  • The full trip. Flights, pre- and post-cruise hotels, transfers and shore excursions handled together.
  • Protection & support. TICO coverage and a real person to call if plans change.

When should I book a cruise?

Popular sailings — especially Alaska, holiday-week Caribbean and river cruises — sell out months ahead, and the best cabins go first. Booking 6–12 months in advance typically gives the widest choice and strongest promotions, while deposits are usually refundable up to a point. Tell Madz your destination and dates and we'll advise the ideal booking window.

Is a cruise good for first-time travellers?

Absolutely — cruises are one of the easiest, most reassuring ways to travel. Everything is included and organized, you only unpack once, and there's something for every age on board. We'll steer first-timers toward the ships and itineraries that make the smoothest introduction.
